# MyEvidence
For clinicians who love Pubmed, but hate having 37 tabs open and trying to track what they've read... who love guidelines, but can't read through 80-page PDFs to find the parts relevant to them.

## What you can do
- Build a repository of pertinent abstracts and guidelines.
- Save PubMed abstracts by PMID. The app auto-extracts key details.
- Upload guideline PDFs. The app extracts recommendations and organizes them into categories.
- Search and browse your database.
- Search PubMed and track your progress with a ledger.
- Use your database to ask AI focused questions without dilution from the whole internet.

## Pages
- **PMID → Abstract**: Enter a PMID, hit fetch. The app pulls the abstract and extracts PICO details, patient count, author's conclusion, and specialty. Edit anything before saving. Related papers show up via PubMed and Semantic Scholar, and there's a clipboard to queue up PMIDs you want to check next.
- **Guidelines (PDF Upload)**: Upload a guideline PDF. Title, year, and specialty are auto-extracted. Recommendations get pulled out and sorted into categories, with likely duplicates pushed to the end. You can tweak the display, but be careful not to break formatting.
- **Single-study view**: Look at one record in full detail. Search for it by text, edit metadata, see related studies, and link it to guidelines.
- **Browse studies**: Scroll through everything you've saved. Filter by specialty, year, or search text. Toggle between specialty-grouped and year-grouped views. Add stuff to the evidence cart for batch use later.
- **Generate meta**: Pick an evidence base (from your cart or a saved folder) and ask AI a focused question. Folders let you group related papers so you don't have to re-select them every time.
- **Search PubMed**: Pick a year, month, specialty, and journal — see all results. Journals and study types are limited to high-impact and high-evidence ones on purpose. Mark papers as irrelevant or send them to the PMID → Abstract page. The ledger tracks what you've already searched.
- **Manage**: Delete abstracts, guidelines, or folders. Also where you go to edit metadata after the fact.
- **About**: This page.
- **History**: Timeline of everything you've added, sorted newest first.

### Rapid Reference
- **RRT**: Quick-reference for rapid response scenarios. Medications, dosing, procedures, and key extension numbers.
- **Bedside**: Bedside reference for non-urgent scenarios. Includes various physical exams, for instance.
- **Reminders**: Reference for classically difficult to remember facts. Saves a google search.

### Personalized Pathways
- **Pathways**: Write your own admission plans and daily progress plans. Drag in abstracts and guidelines, and the app auto-sorts them by evidence strength (guidelines first, then RCTs, cohort studies, etc.). Ties are broken by year, patient count, and multicenter status. To ensure you don't forget things while writing notes and entering orders.

## Evidence Workflows
1. **Research a topic from one strong seed paper**
   - Find a relevant article in PubMed and copy its PMID.
   - Open **PMID → Abstract**, fetch the paper, review the extracted fields, and save it to your database.
   - Use **PubMed Related articles** and **Semantic Scholar similar papers** to branch outward.
   - Use the **Clipboard** expander to keep track of PMIDs you want to open next.
   - Repeat with a new seed paper

2. **Get up to date on recent research**
   - Open **Search PubMed**.
   - Review the ledger to see which month/specialty/journal combinations you already searched.
   - Search uncovered gaps, then send promising papers to **PMID → Abstract** and save what matters. Clear what doesn't.

3. **Answer a focused clinical question**
   - Start in **Browse studies** and add relevant abstracts/guidelines to the evidence cart.
   - Go to **Generate meta**, review the cart, and ask your focused question.
   - Alternate path: start in **Generate meta** by adding an evidence folder first, then return to **Browse studies** to add more papers to the cart.
